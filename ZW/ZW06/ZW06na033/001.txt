ZW06na033_p0001a23║文大藏經，但幸而保存在敦煌遺書中。《藏外佛教文獻》
ZW06na033_p0001a24║第三輯發表的《因緣心論頌》、《因緣心論釋》即屬此類。
ZW06na033_p0001a25║第二次發生在西夏，當時把一大批藏文佛典翻譯為漢

ZW06na033_p0002a02║大藏經，但近代以來在西夏故地屢有發現。《藏外佛教

ZW06na033_p0002a06║數量不多，且已入藏。第四次發生在近代，這一次的典
ZW06na033_p0002a07║籍交涉，無論是深度還是廣度，都大大超出前三次。翻
ZW06na033_p0002a08║譯的典籍無慮百十部，但至今缺乏全面、系統的收集與
ZW06na033_p0002a09║整理。我們從中選擇了隆蓮法師於五十年代初期翻譯
ZW06na033_p0002a10║的《入菩薩行論廣解》，刊登在第四輯，以饗讀者。隆蓮
ZW06na033_p0002a11║法師是中國當代比丘尼中的第一人，她的這個譯本，趙

ZW06na033_p0002a14║們希望本文獻的發表能促使研修者更加重視藏傳佛教
ZW06na033_p0002a15║原始資料的學習與鑽研，以正本清源。
ZW06na033_p0002a16║其次是漢譯南傳佛典。古代，曾有不少南傳佛典的

ZW06na033_p0002a20║藏》中的若干南傳佛典，實際是從日文轉譯的。其後，南
ZW06na033_p0002a21║傳佛典的翻譯一直是我國佛教界、學術界注目的重點，
ZW06na033_p0002a22║也翻譯了如《法句經》、《清淨道論》等一些典籍，但從總
ZW06na033_p0002a23║體看，這一工作斷斷續續，沒有能夠真正展開。八十年
ZW06na033_p0002a24║代以來，情況有所改觀，台灣佛教界致力於從日文轉譯
ZW06na033_p0002a25║南傳佛典，大陸學者則努力從巴利語原典直接進行翻
ZW06na033_p0003a01║譯。其中特別是鄧殿臣教授發大願心，把全部力量投入
ZW06na033_p0003a02║這一事業，精心翻譯出一批南傳典籍。正當我們為南傳
ZW06na033_p0003a03║佛典陸續譯出而歡欣的時候，鄧殿臣教授因積勞成疾，
ZW06na033_p0003a04║不幸逝世。這是我們的一大損失。鄧殿臣教授逝世前
ZW06na033_p0003a05║不久，我與他已就在《藏外佛教文獻》上發表他的譯作達
ZW06na033_p0003a06║成一致意見。在與鄧殿臣教授進行遺體告別時，我暗下
ZW06na033_p0003a07║決心，一定要盡力把鄧殿臣教授翻譯的典籍出版出來。
ZW06na033_p0003a08║在鄧殿臣夫人趙桐女士的大力支持下，這一願望終於實
ZW06na033_p0003a09║現。這次刊登在第五輯上的《小誦》、《即興自說》、《大隧

ZW06na033_p0003a12║授翻譯的《大隧道本生經傳》已由中國佛教協會列入「法
ZW06na033_p0003a13║音文庫」印行，《長老偈．長老尼偈》也已經由中國社會科
ZW06na033_p0003a14║學出版社出版。當前，中國佛教界一批年輕僧人對於初
ZW06na033_p0003a15║期佛教持有特殊而又濃厚的興趣，希望從初期佛教中找
ZW06na033_p0003a16║到對治當今佛教界某些弊病的法門。我們希望南傳佛
ZW06na033_p0003a17║教典籍的翻譯，對他們的這種探索能夠有所助益。
ZW06na033_p0003a18║使我們感到特別高興的是我們在第六輯中能夠向
ZW06na033_p0003a19║諸位提供一批雲南阿吒力教的典籍。
ZW06na033_p0003a20║雲南大理等地區流傳著一種稱為「阿吒力教」的佛
ZW06na033_p0003a21║教，這是人們早就知道的事實。但這種阿吒力教究竟屬
ZW06na033_p0003a22║於那一個系統，過去則有著種種不同的說法，有的認為
ZW06na033_p0003a23║它屬於印度密教系統；有的認為它屬於藏傳佛教系統；
ZW06na033_p0003a24║有的認為它是印度密教的本土化，稱之為「滇密」。總
ZW06na033_p0003a25║之，雖然尚不能正確地定義，但一般均將它歸為密教。
ZW06na033_p0004a01║前些年，台灣藍吉富教授在實地調查的基礎上，指出：阿
ZW06na033_p0004a02║吒力教「與密教之間的關係，必須要重新估量。輕率地
ZW06na033_p0004a03║將它與唐代開元間的密教、或日本、西藏的密教（或密
ZW06na033_p0004a04║宗）劃上等號，是不精確的。」他認為：「阿吒力教信
ZW06na033_p0004a05║仰，是仍在雜密階段的法術式的信仰。……似乎祇是密
ZW06na033_p0004a06║教外殼的展示而已，並沒有吸收到密教的核心內涵。
ZW06na033_p0004a07║……阿吒力教的教法，主要仍然來自中國的漢地佛
ZW06na033_p0004a08║教。」為阿吒力教的研究指出了新的方向。
ZW06na033_p0004a09║雲南社會科學院宗教研究所的侯沖先生經過長期
ZW06na033_p0004a10║艱苦的努力，收集到大量的阿吒力教的典籍，在充分佔
ZW06na033_p0004a11║有資料的基礎上，提出阿吒力教並非前人所謂的密教，
ZW06na033_p0004a12║而是在明初教、禪、講分離政策下出現並遷移到雲南的
ZW06na033_p0004a13║「教」派的殘餘。也就是說，所謂阿吒力教，實際是明代
ZW06na033_p0004a14║「教」派的活化石。當然，「活化石」言者，無非是強調阿
ZW06na033_p0004a15║吒力教實際與明代的「教」派一脈相承，並不是說阿吒力
ZW06na033_p0004a16║教完全保持著明代「教」派的原狀，沒有任何改變。諸法
ZW06na033_p0004a17║無常，世界上沒有永不改變的事物。例如由在家人出任
ZW06na033_p0004a18║阿吒力，就是一個巨大的變化。總之，侯沖先生的研究
ZW06na033_p0004a19║雖然還有不少方面需要進一步完善與仔細論證，但已使
ZW06na033_p0004a20║我們對阿吒力教有了全新的認識，以前的疑滯一掃而
ZW06na033_p0004a21║空。
ZW06na033_p0005a01║侯沖先生工作的另一個意義是再次證明了科學研
ZW06na033_p0005a02║究中努力佔有原始資料的重要性。前此人們對於阿吒
ZW06na033_p0005a03║力教所以有種種不著邊際的看法，主要原因是沒有佔有
ZW06na033_p0005a04║原始資料，僅滿足於二手資料的轉抄與人云亦云的摘
ZW06na033_p0005a05║引。藍吉富先生作了實地調查，雖然由於條件有限，沒
ZW06na033_p0005a06║有能夠實際見到多少阿吒力教的典籍，但僅憑他見到的
ZW06na033_p0005a07║部分阿吒力教典籍的目錄及其他資料，他就敏銳地發現

ZW06na033_p0005a15║石刻資料始終是佛教資料的一個重要組成部分。
ZW06na033_p0005a16║石刻資料的內容非常豐富，諸如碑銘、造像題記、刻經等
ZW06na033_p0005a17║等。古代僧人曾十分重視這些資料的收集與利用，如歷

ZW06na033_p0005a21║刻諸種經文偈頌與造像，為我們瞭解與研究當時的佛教
ZW06na033_p0005a22║提供了重要的資料。例如從第四輯所刊的大足石刻資

ZW06na033_p0006a02║敦煌遺書中佛教文獻的整理已經成為《藏外佛教文

ZW06na033_p0008a04║In the foreword to the first number of our amtbamtb Buddhist Texts
ZW06na033_p0008a05║ Not Contained in the Tripitaka, I have pointed out that "Here we
ZW06na033_p0008a06║ find, for example, all kinds of Buddhist scriptures translated in
ZW06na033_p0008a07║ modern times from Sanskrit, Pali, Tibetan, Mongolian, etc.; a
ZW06na033_p0008a08║ great amount of Buddhist texts from the Dunhuang Scripture
ZW06na033_p0008a09║ Cave; ancient Buddhist texts which are kept in various libraries
ZW06na033_p0008a10║ and museums and have not yet been included in the Tripitakas of
ZW06na033_p0008a11║ the various eras; Buddhist materials preserved in dynastic
ZW06na033_p0008a12║ histories, local gazetteers, classified reference books and collected
ZW06na033_p0008a13║ works of individual persons; materials on stone and bronze inscriptions;
ZW06na033_p0008a14║ Buddhist works and materials of recent dates. All the
ZW06na033_p0008a15║ above-mentioned works can be called Buddhist texts not contained
ZW06na033_p0008a16║ in the Tripitaka. "Our first three numbers have, relatively speaking,
ZW06na033_p0008a17║ laid stress upon the collation and publication of a number of
ZW06na033_p0008a18║ Buddhist texts among the surviving documents from Dunhuang,
ZW06na033_p0008a19║ and the fourth to sixth numbers, which we are presenting to our
ZW06na033_p0008a20║ readers, have also selected some important Buddhist texts in other
ZW06na033_p0008a21║ areas so that our readers can get more detailed knowledge of the
ZW06na033_p0008a22║ so-called Buddhist texts not contained in the Tripitaka.
ZW06na033_p0008a23║Let me first talk about the Chinese translations of the Tibetan
ZW06na033_p0008a24║ Buddhist scriptures. As is well known, Tibetan Buddhism,
ZW06na033_p0008a25║ being one of the three major Buddhist systems in China, and Chinese
ZW06na033_p0008a26║ Buddhism have mutually influenced each other all the time.
ZW06na033_p0008a27║ According to the history of texts exchanges, Tibetan Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0009a01║ texts have exerted influence upon Chinese ones four times. The
ZW06na033_p0009a02║ earliest influence occurred during the Tang Dynasty. A number of
ZW06na033_p0009a03║ Tibetan Buddhist texts were translated into Chinese in Dunhuang
ZW06na033_p0009a04║ in more than seventy years when it was under the reign of ancient
ZW06na033_p0009a05║ Tibet named Tubo. Although the number of the translations was
ZW06na033_p0009a06║ not large, they had influenced Dunhuang Buddhism greatly. Nevertheless,
ZW06na033_p0009a07║ these texts have not reached the interior of our country,
ZW06na033_p0009a08║ and as a result, have not been included into Chinese Tripitakas.
ZW06na033_p0009a09║ Fortunately, they have been preserved among the surviving Dunhuang
ZW06na033_p0009a10║ documents. The two texts entitled Yin Yuan Xin Lun Song
ZW06na033_p0009a11║ and Yin Yuan Xin Lun Shi, which we have published in the third
ZW06na033_p0009a12║ number of our Buddhist Texts Not Contained in the Tripitaka,
ZW06na033_p0009a13║ just fall into this category. The later influence occurred during the
ZW06na033_p0009a14║ reign of the Western Xia, when a good number of Tibetan Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0009a15║ scriptures were translated into Chinese. Those Chinese texts
ZW06na033_p0009a16║ mainly spread in the area controlled by the Western Xia regime,
ZW06na033_p0009a17║ and therefore have not been included into traditional Tripitakas.
ZW06na033_p0009a18║ However, in places where the Western Xia used to be, people
ZW06na033_p0009a19║ have repeatedly found some of the texts since modern times. One
ZW06na033_p0009a20║ example is the Ba Zhong Cu Zhong Fan Duo published in the first
ZW06na033_p0009a21║ number of the Buddhist Texts Not Contained in the Tripitaka.
ZW06na033_p0009a22║ Obviously, the influences exerted during the above-mentioned two
ZW06na033_p0009a23║ times were confined in a very limited area. The following two influences,
ZW06na033_p0009a24║ however, have been much farther and wider. The third
ZW06na033_p0009a25║ influence occurred during the Yuan Dynasty, when, from the material
ZW06na033_p0009a26║ available now, a small number of Buddhist texts were translated
ZW06na033_p0009a27║ into Chinese and included into the Tripitakas. The fourth
ZW06na033_p0009a28║ influence occurred in modern times, when the involved texts, in
ZW06na033_p0010a01║ terms of both depth and width, have greatly surpassed the first
ZW06na033_p0010a02║ three times. The number of the translated texts is about one hundred.
ZW06na033_p0010a03║ Hitherto, they have not been collected and sifted completely
ZW06na033_p0010a04║ and systematically. Now, we have selected the text Ru Pu Sa Xin
ZW06na033_p0010a05║ Lun Guang Shi translated by Master Longlian in early 1950's and
ZW06na033_p0010a06║ would like to offer it to our readers in the forth number of the
ZW06na033_p0010a07║ Buddhist Texts Not Contained in the Tripitaka. Master Longlian
ZW06na033_p0010a08║ has excelled as a bhiksuni in contemporary China, and her translation
ZW06na033_p0010a09║ has won the final judgment by Mr. Zhao Puchu in his preface
ZW06na033_p0010a10║ to it. At present, many people have showed keen interest in
ZW06na033_p0010a11║ Tibetan Tantrism, and quite a number of books in this area have
ZW06na033_p0010a12║ been published. However, among them have been found various
ZW06na033_p0010a13║ views. We hope that the publication of this text here can spur researchers
ZW06na033_p0010a14║ to pay more attention to the studies of the source material
ZW06na033_p0010a15║ of Tibetan Buddhism so as to avoid unnecessary errors.
ZW06na033_p0010a16║Then come the Chinese translated versions of the southern
ZW06na033_p0010a17║ Buddhist scriptures. In ancient times, many a southern Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0010a18║ scripture had been translated into Chinese. However, with the
ZW06na033_p0010a19║ decline of Buddhism, the translation work had been suspended for
ZW06na033_p0010a20║ centuries. When the Puhuizang was being compiled in 1930's,
ZW06na033_p0010a21║ they planned to resume the work to translate some southern Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0010a22║ scriptures into Chinese. Being in short of specialized personnel,
ZW06na033_p0010a23║ some southern Buddhist scriptures, which were included into
ZW06na033_p0010a24║ the Puhuizang, had as a matter of fact been retranslated into Chinese
ZW06na033_p0010a25║ from Japanese. Since then, the translation work of the
ZW06na033_p0010a26║ southern Buddhist scriptures has become a focal point for the Chinese
ZW06na033_p0010a27║ Buddhist and academic circles. Some scriptures such as the
ZW06na033_p0010a28║Fa Ju Jing and the Qing Jing Dao Lun had been translated into
ZW06na033_p0011a01║ Chinese. However, this work, on the whole, being frequently off
ZW06na033_p0011a02║ and on, had not been really unfolded. The situation has changed
ZW06na033_p0011a03║ somewhat since 1980's. The Buddhist circles in Taiwan have
ZW06na033_p0011a04║ been engaged in translating the southern Buddhist scriptures from
ZW06na033_p0011a05║ Japanese while the Buddhist scholars in the mainland have made
ZW06na033_p0011a06║ efforts to translate the scriptures directly from the original Pali
ZW06na033_p0011a07║ texts. What is especially worth mentioning is Prof. Deng Dianchen's
ZW06na033_p0011a08║ great determination and dedication to the cause, into
ZW06na033_p0011a09║ which he had devoted all his efforts. And he had meticulously
ZW06na033_p0011a10║ translated a group of southern Buddhist scriptures. While we were
ZW06na033_p0011a11║ very delighted to see that the southern Buddhist scriptures had
ZW06na033_p0011a12║ been continuously put into Chinese we learned the sad news that
ZW06na033_p0011a13║ Prof. Deng had passed away because of constant overworking.
ZW06na033_p0011a14║ His death was a great loss to our cause. Shortly before his death,
ZW06na033_p0011a15║ Prof. Deng and I had agreed to publish his translations in the
ZW06na033_p0011a16║ Buddhist Texts Not Contained in the Tripitaka. While I was
ZW06na033_p0011a17║ paying my last respects to him I made up my mind in silence to
ZW06na033_p0011a18║ try my best to publish the scriptures that Prof. Deng had translated
ZW06na033_p0011a19║ into Chinese. Now, this wish has come true with the energetic
ZW06na033_p0011a20║ support form Mrs. Zhao Tong, Professor Deng's wife. The Xiao
ZW06na033_p0011a21║ Song, the Ji Xing Zi Shuo and the Da Sui Dao Ben Sheng, which
ZW06na033_p0011a22║ are published in the fifth number of the Buddhist Texts Not Contained
ZW06na033_p0011a23║ in the Tripitaka, are all Prof. Deng's posthumous works,
ZW06na033_p0011a24║ and the Da Nian Chu Jing has been jointly translated by Prof.
ZW06na033_p0011a25║ Deng and Mrs. Zhao. In addition, the Da Sui Dao Ben Sheng
ZW06na033_p0011a26║ Jing Zhuan translated by Prof. Deng has been published and included
ZW06na033_p0011a27║ into the "Library of Dharma Voice" by the Chinese Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0011a28║ Association, and his Zhang Lao Ji and Zhang Lao Ni Ji
ZW06na033_p0012a01║ have also been put into one book and published by the Chinese Social
ZW06na033_p0012a02║ Sciences Publishing House. At present, a group of young
ZW06na033_p0012a03║ monks in Chinese Buddhist circles have been greatly interested in
ZW06na033_p0012a04║ initial Buddhism, and expect to find ways from early Buddhism to
ZW06na033_p0012a05║ cure some maladies in contemporary Buddhist circles. We hope
ZW06na033_p0012a06║ that the translations of the southern Buddhist scriptures can be
ZW06na033_p0012a07║ helpful to their exploration.
ZW06na033_p0012a08║What has extremely delighted us is that we are able to offer a
ZW06na033_p0012a09║ group of scriptures of the Azhali Sect in Yunnan to our readers.
ZW06na033_p0012a10║The Buddhist Azhali Sect is popular in Dali and some other
ZW06na033_p0012a11║ areas in Yunnan. This has been a well-known fact for a long
ZW06na033_p0012a12║ time. Nevertheless, as to which system this Azhali Sect actually
ZW06na033_p0012a13║ belongs to, people have voiced various opinions. Some think that
ZW06na033_p0012a14║ it belongs to the system of Indian Tantrism; some feel that it belongs
ZW06na033_p0012a15║ to the system of Tibetan Buddhism; others hold that it is
ZW06na033_p0012a16║ the result of the naturalization of Indian Tantrism, and therefore
ZW06na033_p0012a17║ name it as "Yunnan Tantrism." In a word, although people are
ZW06na033_p0012a18║ still unable to define it properly, they generally attribute it to
ZW06na033_p0012a19║ Tantrism. A few years ago, Prof. Lan Jifu from Taiwan, after
ZW06na033_p0012a20║ his on-the-spot investigation there, pointed out that "the relationships
ZW06na033_p0012a21║ between the Azhali Sect and Tantrism has to be reassessed.
ZW06na033_p0012a22║ It would be imprecise to indiscreetly equate the Azhali Sect with
ZW06na033_p0012a23║ the Tang Dynasty Tantrism during the reign period named
ZW06na033_p0012a24║ Kaiyuan (713-741) or Japanese and Tibetan Tantrism. "(Yunnan
ZW06na033_p0012a25║ Dali De Azhali Jiao, Zhongguo Fojiao Fanlun, Xin Wen
ZW06na033_p0012a26║ Feng Publishing Company, August 1993, p. 5) He thought that
ZW06na033_p0012a27║ "the Azhali belief is still the magic belief at the mixed period of
ZW06na033_p0012a28║ Tantrism...It seems to be just the show of the outer casting of
ZW06na033_p0013a01║ Tantrism, and has not yet absorbed the kernel connotation of
ZW06na033_p0013a02║ Tantrism...The doctrine of the Azhali Sect has mainly come
ZW06na033_p0013a03║ from hinterland Buddhism of China." (ibid., p. 71) He has indicated
ZW06na033_p0013a04║ a new orientation for the studies of the Azhali Sect. Mr.
ZW06na033_p0013a05║ Hou Chong, who works with the Institute of Religious Studies,
ZW06na033_p0013a06║ the Yunnan Social Sciences Academy, has collected a large
ZW06na033_p0013a07║ amount of the scriptures of the Azhali Sect after long-termed
ZW06na033_p0013a08║ painstaking efforts. He has put forth on the basis of his rich material
ZW06na033_p0013a09║ that the Azhali Sect is not equal to Tantrism as our predecessors
ZW06na033_p0013a10║ have stated. On the contrary, it is in fact the vestiges of the
ZW06na033_p0013a11║ "religious" sect that occurred and migrated to Yunnan in early
ZW06na033_p0013a12║ Ming Dynasty. In another word, the so-called Azhali Sect is, as a
ZW06na033_p0013a13║ matter of fact, a living fossil of the "religious" sect in the Ming
ZW06na033_p0013a14║ Dynasty. Of course, the living fossil here means to stress that the
ZW06na033_p0013a15║ origin of the Azhali Sect can be traced back to the "religious" sect
ZW06na033_p0013a16║ in the Ming Dynasty. By saying so, we do not mean that the
ZW06na033_p0013a17║ Azhali Sect is identical with the "religious" sect in the Ming Dynasty
ZW06na033_p0013a18║ and had not undergone any changes. All dharmas are
ZW06na033_p0013a19║ changeable, and nothing can remain intact in the world. For instance,
ZW06na033_p0013a20║ it is a great change for a layman to work for the Azhali
ZW06na033_p0013a21║ Sect at present. In a word, Mr. Hou's research has cleared all
ZW06na033_p0013a22║ kinds of doubts and helped us gain completely new knowledge of
ZW06na033_p0013a23║ Azhali Sect, though there is still many an aspect in his research
ZW06na033_p0013a24║ work for him to improve and perfect.
ZW06na033_p0013a25║It is also significant for Mr. Hou to prove once again in his
ZW06na033_p0013a26║ work that it is of great importance to try to gain firsthand information
ZW06na033_p0013a27║ in scientific research. That people had various impractical
ZW06na033_p0013a28║ opinions on the Azhali Sect before was mainly due to their lack of
ZW06na033_p0014a01║ source material and their satisfaction with copying and citing some
ZW06na033_p0014a02║ secondhand information. Mr. Lan made on-the-spot investigation
ZW06na033_p0014a03║ and yet, confined by conditions, was unable to practically see
ZW06na033_p0014a04║ many Azhali scriptures. However, just by the catalogs of partial
ZW06na033_p0014a05║ Azhali scriptures and other material he had come across, he keenly
ZW06na033_p0014a06║ found out that the before-going viewpoints were mistaken. The
ZW06na033_p0014a07║ key for Mr. Hou to discover what others had not is his investigation,
ZW06na033_p0014a08║ obtainment and collation of a large amount of Azhali scriptures
ZW06na033_p0014a09║ that our predecessors had not seen. What we publish in the
ZW06na033_p0014a10║ sixth number of our Buddhist Texts Not Contained in the Tripitaka
ZW06na033_p0014a11║ is just a part of them. We do hope that this kind of style of
ZW06na033_p0014a12║ study, which attaches importance to source material, can be carried
ZW06na033_p0014a13║ forward in reality. At the same time, we also hope that various
ZW06na033_p0014a14║ working places that collect and store documents can inherit
ZW06na033_p0014a15║ the good tradition to regard "learning as a public business under
ZW06na033_p0014a16║ the heaven," and make due contributions to the development of
ZW06na033_p0014a17║ Chinese academic and cultural causes.
ZW06na033_p0014a18║Stone inscriptions have always been a very important part of
ZW06na033_p0014a19║ Buddhist material. The content of stone inscriptions has been
ZW06na033_p0014a20║ pretty rich and full, covering tablet inscriptions, tablet records of
ZW06na033_p0014a21║ statue construction and scriptures carved on stone blocks, etc..
ZW06na033_p0014a22║ Ancient monks once paid exceptional attention to the collection
ZW06na033_p0014a23║ and utilization of the material. For instance, almost all the biographies
ZW06na033_p0014a24║ of the monks in the past ages made wide use of the
ZW06na033_p0014a25║ stone inscriptions. In the fourth number of the Buddhist Texts
ZW06na033_p0014a26║ Not Contained in the Tripitaka, we publish a group of the stone
ZW06na033_p0014a27║ inscriptions from Dazu, Sichuan. The Dazu Grottoes opened up in
ZW06na033_p0014a28║ the Song Dynasty are among the famous Chinese grottoes. All the
ZW06na033_p0015a01║ carved scriptures, hymns and statues have offered important material
ZW06na033_p0015a02║ for us to get to know and make studies of the then Buddhism.
ZW06na033_p0015a03║ The Dazu stone inscriptions published in the fourth number
ZW06na033_p0015a04║ of the Buddhist Texts Not Contained in the Tripitaka, for instance,
ZW06na033_p0015a05║ have clearly reflected the features of the then Buddhism:
ZW06na033_p0015a06║ to encourage people to do good and punish evil-doers by the concept
ZW06na033_p0015a07║ of retribution; to win over people by advocating filial duty;
ZW06na033_p0015a08║ and to call on people to get salvation from other forces so as to obtain
ZW06na033_p0015a09║ their deliverance, which are just the three most fundamental
ZW06na033_p0015a10║ characteristics of the popularized Chinese Buddhism or Buddhism
ZW06na033_p0015a11║ in the nature of belief.
ZW06na033_p0015a12║Collating Buddhist texts from the surviving Dunhuang books
ZW06na033_p0015a13║ has become a distinguishing feature of the Buddhist Texts Not
ZW06na033_p0015a14║ Contained in the Tripitaka. Since our three numbers from the
ZW06na033_p0015a15║ fourth to the sixth have included a lot of non-Dunhuang material,
ZW06na033_p0015a16║ we have to reduce the scope for Dunhuang material and can only
ZW06na033_p0015a17║ publish five texts concerned. The fourth number also publishes
ZW06na033_p0015a18║ two pieces of material regarding the Sanjie Sect. One of them is
ZW06na033_p0015a19║ entitled Fo Xing Wen Da, discovered in the Beijing Library in recent
ZW06na033_p0015a20║ years, and we have specially requested Mr. Ye Luhua to collate
ZW06na033_p0015a21║ it. Since there are more nonstandard forms of Chinese characters
ZW06na033_p0015a22║ used in Dunhuang in the text, and we lack any collated texts
ZW06na033_p0015a23║ for us to check against, we can not say for certain that we have identified
ZW06na033_p0015a24║ all the Chinese characters without any mistakes, though
ZW06na033_p0015a25║ we have rectified the text repeatedly and have also consulted some
ZW06na033_p0015a26║ experts in philology. We, therefore, request our readers to point
ZW06na033_p0015a27║ out any mistakes they may find. Another text entitled Da Cheng
ZW06na033_p0015a28║ Wu Jin Zang Fa is an early work by Xinxing. The Japanese
ZW06na033_p0016a01║ scholar 矢吹慶輝 once named it as "Posthumous Work by
ZW06na033_p0016a02║ Xinxing" and included it into the Sanjiejiao Yanjiu as its
ZW06na033_p0016a03║ material. Now it has been collated once again according to some
ZW06na033_p0016a04║ newly-found material. The Ru Lai Zai Jin Guan Zhu Lei Qing
ZW06na033_p0016a05║ Jing Zhuang Yan Jing Fu Jing is a "doubtful and spurious work"
ZW06na033_p0016a06║ written by a Chinese, and we have collated it on the basis of some
ZW06na033_p0016a07║ surviving Dunhuang books and some stone inscription material.
ZW06na033_p0016a08║ The said text emphasizes particularly that one must write a scripture
ZW06na033_p0016a09║ or build a statue according to dharma; it can also reflect the
ZW06na033_p0016a10║ great efforts the monks in the Northern and Southern Dynasties
ZW06na033_p0016a11║ had made to correct the maladies of the times. I think that it is of
ZW06na033_p0016a12║ certain significance for every collator of the Dunhuang Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0016a13║ literature to learn something from this. The Dunhuang Buddhist
ZW06na033_p0016a14║ texts in the three said numbers also include the Yu Jia Shi Di Kai
ZW06na033_p0016a15║ Shi Fen Men Ji in the fifth number and the Jin Xin Yi Da Fang
ZW06na033_p0016a16║ Guang Fo Hua Yan Jing Biao (Fu Zong Mu) in the sixth
ZW06na033_p0016a17║ number.
ZW06na033_p0016a18║Each number from the fourth to the sixth publishes a research
ZW06na033_p0016a19║ paper. Mr. Chen Jinhua's paper entitled Chuan Shanwuwei

ZW06na033_p0016a26║ former. Since this has involved a series of vital matters with both

ZW06na033_p0017a05║ Sect. We therefore have attached it as an appendix to the texts of
ZW06na033_p0017a06║ the Azhali Sect published in our sixth number. As for my paper
